Local SEO is essential for businesses looking to reach customers in their region. Whether you have a physical store or provide services in a specific area - local visibility is crucial. In this guide, you will learn everything about local SEO.

## What is Local SEO?

Local SEO focuses on improving your visibility for searches with local intent:

- "[service] + [city]" (web design rotterdam)
- "[service] near me"
- "[product] near me"

### Local Search Results

Google displays local results in two ways:

**1. Local Pack (Map Pack)**
The map with 3 businesses that appears for local searches.

**2. Organic Local Results**
Regular search results with local relevance.

### Why Local SEO Matters

- 46% of all Google searches have local intent
- 76% of local searches lead to a store visit within 24 hours
- 28% of local searches result in a purchase

## Google Business Profile

Your Google Business Profile (formerly Google My Business) is the most important element of local SEO.

### Creating/Claiming Your Profile

1. Go to business.google.com
2. Log in with your Google account
3. Search for your business or create a new one
4. Verify via postcard, phone, or email

### Optimizing Your Profile

**Basic information:**
- [ ] Official business name (do not add keywords)
- [ ] Correct address
- [ ] Phone number
- [ ] Website URL
- [ ] Opening hours

**Category:**
- [ ] Choose primary category (most specific)
- [ ] Add secondary categories
- [ ] Maximum 10 categories

**Description:**
- [ ] 750 characters available
- [ ] Include keywords naturally
- [ ] Mention unique selling points
- [ ] Call-to-action

**Photos:**
- [ ] Logo
- [ ] Cover photo
- [ ] Exterior photos
- [ ] Interior photos
- [ ] Team photos
- [ ] Product/service photos

**Attributes:**
- [ ] Select relevant attributes
- [ ] Payment methods
- [ ] Accessibility
- [ ] Service-specific options

### Publishing Posts

Google Posts are mini-updates on your profile:

**Types:**
- Updates (news, announcements)
- Events (with date)
- Offers (with end date)
- Products

**Best practices:**
- Publish weekly
- Add images
- Use call-to-action buttons
- Keep it short and relevant

### Questions & Answers

Monitor and answer questions:
- Respond quickly
- Be helpful
- Post frequently asked questions yourself and answer them

## Reviews Strategy

Reviews are a crucial ranking factor for local SEO.

### Collecting Reviews

**Actively ask for reviews:**
- After successful transaction
- Via email follow-up
- With review cards
- Via website link

**Make it easy:**
Direct link to review form:
```
https://search.google.com/local/writereview?placeid=YOUR_PLACE_ID
```

### Responding to Reviews

**Positive reviews:**
- Thank the customer
- Personalize the response
- Mention specific details

**Negative reviews:**
- Respond quickly and professionally
- Take responsibility
- Offer a solution
- Take the conversation offline if necessary

### Tips for Generating Reviews

- Timing is key (right after a positive experience)
- Make it personal
- Do not follow up with repeated requests
- Never buy fake reviews

## NAP Consistency

NAP stands for Name, Address, Phone number. Consistency is essential.

### Why It Matters

Google verifies your business information by comparing sources. Inconsistencies cause confusion and lower rankings.

### NAP Audit

Check your information on:
- Google Business Profile
- Website (footer, contact page)
- Social media profiles
- Business directories
- Review sites

### Common Issues

**Inconsistent spelling:**
```
✗ Robuust Marketing BV
✗ Robuust Marketing B.V.
✗ Robuust Marketing
✓ Choose one spelling and use it everywhere
```

**Outdated information:**
- Old addresses after moving
- Old phone numbers
- Old trade names

### NAP Implementation

**On your website:**
```html
<script type="application/ld+json">
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "LocalBusiness",
  "name": "Robuust Marketing",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "Street Name 123",
    "addressLocality": "The Hague",
    "postalCode": "1234 AB",
    "addressCountry": "NL"
  },
  "telephone": "+31-XX-XXXXXXX"
}
</script>
```

## Local Citations

Citations are mentions of your business on other websites.

### Important Citation Sources

**General directories:**
- Google Business Profile
- Bing Places
- Apple Maps
- Yelp

**Local/Regional directories:**
- Chamber of Commerce register
- Industry associations
- Local business directories

**Industry-specific:**
- Depending on your sector

### Building Citations

1. Start with the major platforms
2. Add industry-specific directories
3. Ensure consistent NAP
4. Add additional information where possible

### Citation Management Tools

- BrightLocal
- Moz Local
- Yext (enterprise)

## Local Content

### Local Landing Pages

Create a page for each service area:

```
/services/web-design-the-hague
/services/web-design-rotterdam
/services/web-design-amsterdam
```

**Per page:**
- Unique content (not just replacing the city name)
- Local references
- Local testimonials if possible
- Embedded Google Maps

### Local Content Ideas

**Blog posts:**
- "Best [service] in [city]"
- Local news/events
- Case studies with local businesses
- Collaborations with local organizations

**Local guides:**
- "Complete guide to [topic] in [city]"
- "Top 10 [industry] businesses in [region]"

### Local Keywords

**Structure:**
- [service] + [city]
- [service] + [region/state]
- [service] + "near me"
- best [service] + [city]

**Examples:**
- web design rotterdam
- SEO specialist south holland
- website development near me

## Technical Local SEO

### Local Schema Markup

```json
{
  "@context": "https://schema.org",
  "@type": "LocalBusiness",
  "name": "Robuust Marketing",
  "image": "https://robuustmarketing.nl/logo.png",
  "@id": "https://robuustmarketing.nl",
  "url": "https://robuustmarketing.nl",
  "telephone": "+31-XX-XXXXXXX",
  "address": {
    "@type": "PostalAddress",
    "streetAddress": "Street Name 123",
    "addressLocality": "The Hague",
    "postalCode": "1234 AB",
    "addressCountry": "NL"
  },
  "geo": {
    "@type": "GeoCoordinates",
    "latitude": 52.0705,
    "longitude": 4.3007
  },
  "openingHoursSpecification": [{
    "@type": "OpeningHoursSpecification",
    "dayOfWeek": ["Monday", "Tuesday", "Wednesday", "Thursday", "Friday"],
    "opens": "09:00",
    "closes": "17:00"
  }],
  "sameAs": [
    "https://www.facebook.com/robuustmarketing",
    "https://www.linkedin.com/company/robuustmarketing"
  ]
}
```

## Common Mistakes

### 1. Keywords in Business Name

Google can suspend you for keyword stuffing in your business name.

### 2. Fake Reviews

Against Google's guidelines and leads to suspension.

### 3. Inconsistent NAP

Causes confusion for Google and users.

### 4. Outdated Information

Wrong opening hours or address damages trust.

### 5. Duplicate Location Content

Avoid copy-paste content with just the city name replaced.

## Conclusion

Local SEO requires consistent attention:

1. **Foundation:** Optimize Google Business Profile
2. **Reputation:** Actively collect and manage reviews
3. **Consistency:** Keep NAP the same everywhere
4. **Content:** Create relevant local content
5. **Links:** Build local backlinks

Start by optimizing your Google Business Profile and build from there.
